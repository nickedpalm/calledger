## How to Choose a Calibration Laboratory in Fresno

- **Verify the accreditation scope certificate, not just the logo.** ISO/IEC 17025 accreditation means nothing if the parameter you need — say, RF signal generators or high-pressure pneumatic gauges — isn't listed in the lab's A2LA or NVLAP scope. Download the current scope certificate directly from A2LA's directory or NVLAP's lab search before you call.
- **Ask specifically about NIST traceability documentation.** California manufacturing audits (especially aerospace suppliers under AS9100) require calibration certificates that explicitly state the measurement uncertainty and the unbroken chain of traceability back to NIST. If a lab gets cagey about providing that documentation, keep moving.
- **Match the lab to your industry.** Fresno's economy spans food processing equipment, agricultural machinery, aerospace component suppliers, and medical device manufacturers. A lab that's excellent for dimensional calibration of machining tools may not have the thermal or electrical capabilities your biomedical equipment requires. Ask for a recent customer list in your sector if you're unsure.
- **Confirm on-site service availability.** For large fixed equipment — CMMs, environmental chambers, production line scales — you need a lab that dispatches field technicians. Several Fresno-area labs offer on-site calibration across the Central Valley; confirm travel zones and fees upfront.
- **Check turnaround time against your audit calendar.** Standard turnaround at most regional labs is 5–10 business days; rush service (24–72 hours) carries a premium. If you're working toward an ISO 9001 surveillance audit, don't start the calibration cycle less than three weeks out.

> **Pro Tip:** The California Department of Food and Agriculture maintains separate metrology requirements for commercial weighing and measuring devices used in agriculture. If your operation straddles both industrial calibration and CDFA compliance, make sure your lab is authorized for both — they're not automatically the same scope.

## What to Expect

Calibration engagements in Fresno typically run **$500–$5,000**, with the low end covering a handful of hand tools or single instruments and the high end reflecting multi-instrument lab submissions, on-site fieldwork, or complex RF/dimensional equipment requiring extended measurement time. Most labs quote per instrument or per parameter, so a batch of 40 torque wrenches will cost meaningfully less per unit than submitting them one at a time.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake quality teams make is treating calibration as a commodity and defaulting to the cheapest certificate. A $60 calibration sticker from an unaccredited shop will get your ISO 9001 audit flagged immediately — and the cost of rescheduling an audit or recalling product dwarfs whatever you saved. Accredited costs what it costs.

Expect to receive a calibration certificate within your agreed turnaround window, documenting as-found and as-left conditions, measurement uncertainty, and the reference standards used. Keep these on file — auditors will ask for them.
