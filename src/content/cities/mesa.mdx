## How to Choose a Calibration Laboratory in Mesa

- **Verify accreditation scope before you call.** A2LA and NVLAP publish searchable online directories with each lab's exact calibration parameters and measurement ranges. A lab can be ISO/IEC 17025 accredited for electrical but completely out of scope for dimensional — check the certificate, not the website.
- **Match the lab to your audit standard.** AS9100-certified aerospace suppliers need labs familiar with aerospace traceability requirements. ISO 9001 shops have more flexibility, but either way, ask the lab which audit standards their customers most commonly work to.
- **Ask about on-site calibration.** Mesa's manufacturing footprint includes large CNC equipment and fixed test stands that can't realistically come off the floor. Many accredited labs offer on-site services — confirm whether the mobile team carries the same accreditation scope as the in-house lab.
- **Get the uncertainty budget in writing.** Every calibration certificate should include measurement uncertainty values. If a lab can't tell you their CMC (Calibration and Measurement Capability) for the specific instrument type before you ship it, that's a red flag.
- **Check turnaround against your production schedule.** Standard turnaround in the [Phoenix](/locations/arizona/phoenix/) metro runs 5–10 business days. Rush services (1–3 days) are available but typically add 25–50% to the base cost — factor that in when you're scheduling around a customer audit.

> **Pro Tip:** If you're working toward AS9100 certification or renewal, ask prospective labs for a sample calibration certificate before committing. Auditors will scrutinize the format, the uncertainty statement, and the NIST traceability chain. A lab that can't produce a clean sample certificate on request is one you'll be explaining to your auditor.

## What to Expect

Calibration engagements in Mesa typically run $500–$5,000 depending on instrument count, complexity, and whether you need on-site service or can ship. A single handheld multimeter might cost $75–$150; a torque wrench calibration runs $100–$200; a full CMM calibration or environmental chamber qualification can push into the thousands. Most labs require you to send equipment in a padded, labeled case with a calibration request form specifying the required accuracy class.

> **Reality Check:** Don't compare labs on price alone. A $50 cheaper certificate that's missing a proper uncertainty statement or comes from a lab outside your required accreditation scope will cost you far more when your registrar flags it during audit. The cheapest calibration is the one that actually passes review.
