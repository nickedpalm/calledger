## How to Choose a Calibration Laboratory in San Antonio

- **Verify accreditation scope before anything else.** ISO/IEC 17025 accreditation is the floor, not a differentiator — what matters is the lab's accredited *scope*. A2LA and NVLAP both publish online scope certificates listing the exact parameters, measurement ranges, and uncertainties each lab is authorized to cover. If you're calibrating torque wrenches for an AS9100 audit, confirm the lab's scope explicitly includes torque in your required range. Don't take a sales rep's word for it.

- **Match the lab to your audit driver.** San Antonio's defense contractor ecosystem means a lot of shops are chasing AS9100D and NADCAP approvals — make sure the lab understands aerospace documentation requirements, not just general ISO 9001. Ask specifically whether they've supported AS9100 audits and whether their certificates of calibration include measurement uncertainty statements (they're required; some smaller labs still omit them).

- **Ask about turnaround on calibration urgency.** A broken torque wrench or out-of-cal CMM sitting on your production floor costs real money. Some San Antonio labs offer rush service for 24-48 hour turnaround. Others are scheduling two weeks out. Get this answer before you need it, not after a line goes down.

- **On-site calibration matters for large or fixed equipment.** Labs that only do bench work aren't the right fit if you've got a temperature chamber, CNC machine tool, or fixed pressure system that can't leave the facility. Several local and regional providers serving San Antonio offer mobile calibration teams — confirm the service delivery model matches your equipment.

- **Check for military/DCSA facility clearance if relevant.** If your San Antonio facility handles ITAR-controlled programs, you may need a calibration provider who can work within a cleared environment. Not all labs can. This narrows the field significantly and is worth surfacing early.

> **Pro Tip:** Pull the lab's most recent A2LA or NVLAP scope certificate from the accreditation body's public directory *yourself* — don't rely on a PDF the lab emailed you six months ago. Scope certificates have expiration dates and can be suspended. Thirty seconds of verification protects you from a nonconformance.

## What to Expect

Calibration engagements in San Antonio typically run $500–$5,000 depending on the number of instruments, calibration type (dimensional and electrical jobs price very differently), and whether you need on-site service or are shipping equipment to the lab. Certificates of calibration, NIST-traceable documentation, and measurement uncertainty data should be included as standard deliverables — not add-ons. Standard turnaround is five to ten business days for most bench calibrations; plan for longer if you have specialized metrology needs.

> **Reality Check:** The most common procurement mistake is selecting a lab based on price alone and discovering after the audit that the certificate of calibration lacks the required uncertainty statement or the lab's scope doesn't actually cover your instrument's range. A $200 savings on calibration fees is not worth a major nonconformance finding. Buy accreditation scope first, price second.
