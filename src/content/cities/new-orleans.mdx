## How to Choose a Calibration Laboratory in New Orleans

- **Verify accreditation scope before anything else.** A2LA and NVLAP publish laboratory scope certificates online — download the actual PDF, not just the logo on the website. Confirm the lab is accredited for your specific parameter (dimensional, electrical, pressure, thermal) and that the ranges cover your instruments. A lab accredited for general electrical doesn't automatically cover your high-voltage test equipment.
- **Match the lab to your industry's traceability requirements.** NASA Michoud and the AS9100 suppliers clustered around it need aerospace-grade documentation. A lab that calibrates coffee shop espresso machines and aerospace pressure gauges under the same workflow is a red flag — ask specifically what percentage of their work is AS9100.
- **Ask for a sample calibration certificate before committing.** The certificate is the deliverable. It should include measurement uncertainty, reference standards used, environmental conditions, NIST traceability chain, and the technician's credentials. If they can't produce a sample in 10 minutes, move on.
- **Factor in turnaround against your audit timeline.** Most New Orleans labs quote 5–10 business days standard, 2–3 days rush. If you're two weeks from an audit and have 40 instruments to certify, confirm capacity before submitting — labs serving the offshore oil sector get slammed when rig equipment comes in post-maintenance season.
- **Check whether they do on-site calibration.** For heavy equipment or instruments that can't be moved without compromising calibration — coordinate measurement machines, large torque testers, installed pressure gauges — on-site service isn't optional. Not every accredited lab sends technicians to the field.

> **Pro Tip:** Louisiana's offshore energy sector means labs here are well-versed in API and ASME calibration requirements alongside the standard ISO 17025 framework. If your instruments are used in upstream or midstream applications, ask specifically about their oil and gas client list — it's a legitimate proxy for relevant experience.

## What to Expect

Calibration engagements in New Orleans typically run $500–$5,000 depending on instrument count, calibration type, and whether you need on-site service or expedited turnaround — a single multimeter certificate might run $75 while a full dimensional calibration of 30 instruments ahead of an audit pushes $3,000–$4,500. Most labs require instruments to be dropped off clean and functional; sending in damaged equipment and expecting a calibration certificate back is not how this works.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake quality managers make is shopping only on per-instrument cost and ignoring the certificate format. If your customer or registrar requires a specific data format, uncertainty budget documentation, or a scope statement that matches your product standard, a cheaper lab that can't deliver that format will cost you far more in rejected certificates and re-submittals than the price difference ever saved.
