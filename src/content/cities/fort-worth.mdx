## How to Choose a Calibration Laboratory in Fort Worth

- **Verify the accreditation scope, not just the logo.** Any lab can put "ISO/IEC 17025 accredited" on their website. What matters is their A2LA or NVLAP scope certificate — a publicly searchable document that lists exactly which parameters (dimensional, electrical, pressure, thermal) and measurement ranges they're authorized to calibrate. If your torque wrench or pressure gauge isn't in scope, the certificate is worthless for your audit.
- **Match the lab to your industry's standard.** Fort Worth is home to major aerospace and defense operations — Bell Textron's headquarters sits right here. If you're in that supply chain, you need a lab with AS9100 audit experience, not just a generic ISO 9001 shop. Ask specifically: "Have you supported AS9100 or NADCAP audits?"
- **Ask about NIST traceability documentation.** Traceability isn't a checkbox — it's a chain. Your calibration certificate should cite the lab's reference standards, which should trace back to NIST (or an equivalent national metrology institute). Get a sample certificate before you commit.
- **Turnaround time is negotiable, but only if you ask.** Standard lead times in this market run 5–15 business days. Rush service (1–3 days) is available at most labs but rarely advertised. If you're pre-audit, ask upfront — don't find out on day 12 that you needed to pay for rush.
- **Check for on-site capability if you have immovable equipment.** Fort Worth manufacturing floors often have CMMs, large-frame fixtures, or environmental chambers that can't leave the building. Several local labs offer mobile calibration teams. Confirm whether they bring their own reference standards or whether you need to arrange that separately.

> **Pro Tip:** Before your first call, pull your equipment list and group instruments by parameter type (dimensional, electrical, etc.). Labs quote faster and more accurately when you send a structured list — and you'll immediately expose any gaps in their accreditation scope.

## What to Expect

Calibration engagements in Fort Worth typically run $500–$5,000 depending on instrument complexity, the number of points calibrated, and whether you need a formal uncertainty budget documented for an audit. A single multimeter calibration might run $75–$150, but an AS9100-required package covering 40 instruments across multiple parameters will land in the mid-four-figures.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is treating calibration like a commodity and shopping purely on per-instrument price. A $40 cheaper certificate that isn't on scope for your parameter, or that lacks proper measurement uncertainty statements, will get flagged by your auditor — and the re-calibration cost plus audit delay will dwarf whatever you saved.
