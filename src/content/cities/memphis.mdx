## How to Choose a Calibration Laboratory in Memphis

- **Verify accreditation scope, not just accreditation.** A lab can be ISO/IEC 17025 accredited and still not be authorized to calibrate your torque wrenches or pressure gauges. Pull their A2LA or NVLAP scope certificate directly from the accreditation body's public directory and confirm your specific parameters and measurement ranges are listed. A certificate on the wall means nothing if your instrument type isn't in the scope.
- **Ask for NIST-traceable calibration certificates with uncertainty statements.** Any lab worth hiring will produce a certificate that documents measurement uncertainty in accordance with ILAC-P14. If they hand you a sticker and a one-line printout, that's not a calibration certificate — it's theater.
- **Match the lab's primary industry experience to yours.** Memphis has a heavier medical device and logistics/industrial presence than, say, an aerospace hub. If you're doing AS9100 work on flight-critical tooling, confirm the lab has active aerospace customers and calibrates to those tolerances routinely, not occasionally.
- **Clarify turnaround before you ship anything.** Standard turnaround at most regional labs runs 5–10 business days. Rush service (1–3 days) typically carries a 25–50% premium. If you're up against an audit deadline, get the turnaround commitment in writing — "we'll try" is not a commitment.
- **Check for on-site calibration capability.** For fixed or large equipment, labs that send a technician to your Memphis facility can save significant downtime. Confirm they carry portable reference standards with valid calibration and that their on-site work falls within their accredited scope, not just their general capabilities.

> **Pro Tip:** Tennessee has no state-level calibration licensing requirement, so the only meaningful quality signal is third-party accreditation. If a lab can't point you to a current A2LA, NVLAP, or PJLA accreditation certificate, treat them as unaccredited regardless of what their website says.

## What to Expect

Calibration engagements in Memphis typically run $500–5,000 depending on the number of instruments, complexity of the measurement parameters, and whether you need on-site service or can ship to the lab. A single precision torque wrench might run $75–150; a full calibration event for 30–50 instruments across a quality system audit cycle lands closer to the upper range.

> **Reality Check:** The most common mistake quality managers make is shopping on price per instrument without accounting for certificate quality. A cheaper lab that produces non-conforming certificates — missing uncertainty values, invalid reference standards, out-of-scope parameters — will cost you far more when your registrar flags it during an ISO 9001 or AS9100 audit. The certificate is the deliverable, not the sticker.
