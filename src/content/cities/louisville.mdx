## How to Choose a Calibration Laboratory in Louisville

- **Verify accreditation scope before you send equipment.** ISO/IEC 17025 accreditation is parameter-specific — a lab accredited for electrical calibration may have zero authority to touch your torque wrenches. Pull the lab's A2LA or NVLAP scope certificate (both are publicly searchable) and confirm your specific instrument type and measurement range appear in it. If the sales rep can't produce a current scope doc in under 24 hours, that tells you something.

- **Match the lab's industry experience to yours.** Louisville's economy runs heavy on manufacturing (Ford's Kentucky Truck Plant is the highest-volume truck plant in the world), healthcare (Humana, Norton, Baptist Health), and aerospace suppliers. A lab that primarily serves semiconductor fabs may not understand the AS9100 audit trail a Tier 1 auto supplier needs. Ask for customer references in your sector.

- **Ask explicitly about NIST traceability documentation.** Every calibration certificate should include an unbroken traceability chain back to NIST. "We're traceable" is a marketing claim. A certificate with specific reference standard IDs and calibration dates is evidence.

- **Clarify turnaround — and what "rush" actually costs.** Standard turnaround in this market runs 5–10 business days. If you're pre-audit or post-repair, you may need 2–3 days. Most labs charge 25–50% premium for expedited service; some won't do it at all for complex dimensional work. Know this before you're staring down an audit date.

- **Check for on-site capability if your equipment can't travel.** Large CMMs, environmental chambers, and installed pressure systems often can't leave the floor. Louisville labs vary significantly in mobile calibration capability — not all of them will send a tech to your facility.

> **Pro Tip:** Kentucky participates in several interstate quality consortiums, and Louisville-area labs occasionally share calibration services under sub-contracting arrangements. If your certificate arrives with a lab name you didn't hire, ask for the sub-contractor's own accreditation documentation. You're entitled to it.

## What to Expect

A typical calibration engagement in Louisville runs $500–$5,000 depending on instrument complexity, quantity, and whether on-site service is required — a single multimeter recalibration sits at the low end; a full metrology lab teardown for an ISO 9001 surveillance audit pushes the ceiling. Most labs quote per-instrument with volume discounts kicking in at 10+ pieces. Standard turnaround is 5–10 business days with certificates delivered digitally.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is assuming the quote covers everything. Ask specifically about as-found/as-left data (sometimes billed separately), certificate storage fees, and whether out-of-tolerance findings trigger an automatic re-calibration charge or just a documented finding. Surprises show up on the back end of invoices, not the front.
